Summary

  • This project is based on data we found on public school students’ test scores across the subjects of math, reading, and writing. With a dataset of 1,000 students recorded, each student is broken down into various categories such as gender, race/ethnicity, whether they attended test preparation, and parental factors. Our project is to create a machine learning model that can predict each students’ test score depending on the features given. We decided to use Logistic Regression in efforts to most accurately predict the student’s test score for each subject.

    Under the Visuals/Graphics tab above, you will see an option to view our entire dataset, as well as a Tableau visuals dashboard. Within the data, we discovered the following findings:

    1. The higher a student’s parental education was, the higher test score the student achieved across most subjects.
    2. While we do not know which groups represent which ethnicity/race, groups D & E both scored the highest on each subject test scores.
    3. Males averaged a higher test score than females across all three subjects, including an almost 15 point gap in math.
    4. As expected, students that completed a test preparation class averaged around 10 points or better on all three of their test score than students who did not.
